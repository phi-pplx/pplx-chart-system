Audit findings

Rhythm contract holds across all nine cards. Every variant respects head / breath / content / foot. The head pins top, content pins above the foot, and the breath lives in the middle 1fr slot.

Regression caught: type sized in pixels breaks at non-export scales. The first render of this audit had G-display at 84px GT Canon and the quote at 54px italic. At a 250px-wide audit card, both wrapped to 7–8 lines and clipped the footer. The fix was switching every type-size declaration from px to cqi (container query inline units), so type scales with the actual card width. Same archetype works at 250px in the audit and at 1080px on social export.

Regression caught: A bars overlapped the deck. When the 1fr breath row collapses (because hero + content sum exceeds body height), the bar motif climbs up into the deck. Fix: enforce a gap: 4cqi on the body grid so the breath is never zero. Now there's a guaranteed minimum gap between hero-stack and content even when 1fr collapses.

Native aspect ratios produced different hero scales. 1:1 hero = 36cqi (≈389px on 1080 export); 4:5 = 42cqi; 9:16 = 42cqi (narrow but tall, so percent-of-width is small relative to height); 16:9 = 14cqi (paired inline with deck via row-flex). PPLX 2026 mark = 6.8cqi on square/portrait, 3.4cqi on 16:9.

The only structural exception is B (2×2 grid). B has no hero stack — the four cells share the load. The hero-stack row becomes empty (intentionally) and the grid pins above the foot. No regression, just a different occupancy of the same shell.

F wallet is the most format-sensitive. 9:16 requires align-content: space-between on the row container so rows distribute across the full vertical column. The lead row title at 7.2cqi naturally wraps to 2 lines on long names like “Sonar-Reasoning-Pro-High” — the wrap is acceptable when the suffix uses the .soft span for tonal differentiation.
