Layout balancer · edge case stress test · v7

Adversarial inputs vs. the three-knob contract.

Three edge cases that probe at the assumptions baked into fit.js: a long category label in A-card (horizontal-only failure mode), a B-card with sparse data (visual-density vs. DOM-height mismatch), and a header-only card where the original test failed because the title used Mono (the third resolves into archetype G with proper editorial typography). Each row shows four variants of the same edge with the balancer running live. Strategies and verdicts populate after fit.js runs.

Edge 01
A-card · long category labels

Bar-chart category labels of increasing length: short (1.5K), medium (1.5K-tok), long (1.5K-tok budget), extreme (Compressed reasoning at 1.5K-tok budget). Predicted failure: balancer sees no change in CCR (labels don't grow vertically) but visual collisions with adjacent labels invisible to the balancer.

Edge 02
B-card · sparse pin distribution

50-pin chart with varying density: 50 active (normal), 10 active (clustered), 3 active (sparse), 1 active (lone peak). Predicted failure: hero number stays the same size, chart area becomes visually empty even though DOM scrollHeight reports identical content.

Edge 03
G-card · editorial header (degenerate failure, resolved)

The original test (header-only with PPLX Mono title) failed because Mono is utility type. On a card where the title IS the content, the title needs editorial display type. G-card uses GT Canon as title face. Three sub-variants by headline character: display (Narrow Medium, dramatic), editorial (Regular, paragraph-scale), quote (Italic, attributed). The fourth cell shows the original Mono-on-title mistake for comparison.
